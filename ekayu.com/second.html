Thermal

processing

Thermal processing for food preservation is one of the most widely utilized techniques in the food industry. Its main objective is to produce shelf-stable products by reducing the population of microorganisms in trays, containers, or multilayer pouches.

Foods are half-cooked and packaged in a container or pouch before being sterilized. Depending on the product, shelf life can range from 6-18 months. Thermal processing is an effective and efficient method of preserving food, providing meals with extended shelf life.

Commercial

sterilization

Commercial sterilization is a process that ensures product safety and quality by killing all microorganisms and enzymes through a combination of temperature and time. This process is carefully optimized for each product based on its required FO value, meaning that each product can receive the most efficient and effective heat treatment.

Although this thermal treatment kills off any harmful bacteria, it can also lead to significant changes in the product's sensory and nutritional properties, as well as the texture retention during and after the process. That's why to ensure the best results, we constantly monitor the time-temperature combination closely and adjust it as needed.

Freeze

drying

Freeze drying is a process in which a completely frozen sample is placed under a vacuum in order to remove water or other solvents from the sample, allowing the ice to change directly from a solid to a vapour without passing through a liquid phase.

While freeze drying can accomplish many things, its primary goal is to protect the biological and chemical structure of a sample through the process of sublimation. Once complete, the sample can be used for testing, long term preservation, or in the case of food, eaten.

Frozen

Freezing food slows decomposition by turning residual moisture into ice, inhibiting the growth of most bacterial species. The process involves lowering the product temperature generally to -18 oc or below.

The physical state of food material is changed when energy is removed by cooling below freezing temperature. The freezing kinetics is important to preserve the food quality and texture.

Dehydrating

Dehydrating food is a method of food preservation that removes moisture from the food so bacteria, yeast, and mold cannot grow and spoil the food.

The process of food dehydration involves simultaneous transfer of mass and heat within the food and the medium used to transfer energy to the food. In food dehydration methods that supply energy to the food using media other than hot air, air or some other gas may be required to remove moisture from the food.

Storage

Storing foods well means knowing and contrasting the causes of their alteration, which includes microorganisms (bacteria, mold), enzymes, micro-organisms, and unfavourable environmental conditions.

One of the most ideal methods for storing food is Refrigeration. It is a temporary storage method to slow down bacterial proliferation and the alteration of the food.
